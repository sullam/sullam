What are the Rumors?

You might have heard of Fake News, Misinformation, or Rumors are premeating in social media. [1]. Especially in Online settings, information spreads quickly and widely, Rumors or Misinformation have been a serious problem.

The misinformation about Hurricane Sandy with the spread of false images is one of the examples of how rumors affect people and society. Maybe one of the most popular Fake News you might have once encountered is the Video showing Barack Obama, which is in fact an artificial made up Obama.[2] Due to the advanced technology, it has become to a point where it is hard to discern the truthfulness of those fake news. This lights the need of understanding how rumors are formed and spread in online media.

Data Exploration

Terms Explanation

  • Rumor Cascades: The crawled Twitter information can be formed into a network data, where the nodes represents a user ID and a set of tweet/retweets and edges refer to retweeting actions. The authors [3] have computed the retweet paths (tracking how the rumors are being retweeted) and computed the Number of Cascades which represents the number of retweet paths. For more detailed information, please refer to [3]
  • Number of Users: Number of Twitter users associated with the rumor claim.
  • Collected From, Collected By: shows the date information indicating the first date the rumor has been created, and the last date the rumor has appeared.

Analyzing the Homophily and the Degrees

Terms Definition

  • Homophily: Homophily represents how users are politically similar, calculated by the homogeneity between two users i and j as σi=σiσj\sigma_i=\sigma_i\sigma_jσi​=σi​σj​ , where σi\sigma_iσi​ is the user polarity of user i. If the two users are in politically opposite positions, the user homogeneity score is close to -4 and close to 4 if they are in politically similar positions
  • Degrees: Degrees refer to the number of edges. Edges are formed when the retweet/tweet actions are occured.

As you can see in the chart, most of the users associated with the rumors show around 0 or positive values, which indicates that the users associated with the rumors collected may have similar political positions and it might be that rumors are spread within the users who are politically similar.

The degree distribution follows a power-law distribution [4] that exhibits a heavy tail, where top 1% of nodes (users) are connected to more than 227 rumors compared to 55% of nodes having less than 10 neighbors.
